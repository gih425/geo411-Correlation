geo411-Correlation
Correlation Analysis of Gross Domestic Product Per Capita and Life Expectancy Across 47 Countries using RStudio.
Analyzises whether a significant statistical relationship exists between a country's GDP per capita and the life expectancy of its population by using a sample of 47 countries. 
Pearson's and Spearman's correlation tests were used to measure the relationships. Pearson's correlation test measured the linear relationship between GDP and life expectancy values. Spearman's correlation test measured the relationship using its ranked data. Both were tested for statistical significance with the p-value measured at 0.05. 
Both tests confirmed a significant correlation meaning wealthier countries tend to have higher life expectancy than poorer countries. Both p-values were below 0.05 allowing us to reject the null hypothesis that they are not correlated.
Spearman's correlation test is the better test here given GDP's nonlinear distribution. Geographically clustered countries share economic, political and social conditions. This can violate the assumption of independence skewing some results.
